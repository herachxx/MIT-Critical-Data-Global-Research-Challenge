Competition Overview

The International Scholars Research Competition (ISRC) 2026 is a prestigious annual academic competition inviting undergraduate and graduate student researchers from institutions worldwide to present original research across a wide range of disciplines.

ISRC aims to foster academic excellence, cross-cultural collaboration, and scientific communication. Now in its 12th year, ISRC has helped launch the careers of over 14,000 scholars from 80+ countries. The 2026 finals will be held in Geneva, Switzerland, with a hybrid option for finalists unable to attend in person.

Eligibility Requirements

1
Enrollment Status
Currently enrolled as undergraduate (year 2+), master's, or first/second-year doctoral students at an accredited university at the time of abstract submission.
2
Institutional Affiliation
Research must be affiliated with the registered institution. Faculty supervisor name and contact must be provided at submission.
3
Original Work
Research must be original and not previously published in a peer-reviewed journal. Preprints on arXiv, bioRxiv, or SSRN are permitted.
4
Team Size
Individual or team submissions (max 4 members). Each member registers individually; one lead author submits on behalf of the team.

Submission Guidelines

Stage 1 — Abstract (Due March 1, 2026)

  • 500–800 words, structured: Background, Methods, Results, Conclusion
  • Submitted via the ISRC portal in PDF format
  • Must include 5 keywords and a track selection

Stage 2 — Full Paper (Due June 15, 2026, by invitation)

  • Maximum 12,000 words excluding references and appendices
  • Formatted using the ISRC official template (available above)
  • Double-spaced, 12pt, 1-inch margins; APA 7th or Chicago 17th citations
  • Data availability statement required; raw data provided on request
  • Supplementary materials submitted as separate attachments

Judging Criteria

A
Originality & Innovation — 30 pts
Is the research question novel? Does the work make a meaningful contribution? Are the methods or findings innovative?
B
Methodological Rigor — 25 pts
Are methods appropriate and correctly applied? Is the sample/dataset adequate? Are analyses sound?
C
Significance of Findings — 25 pts
Do results have meaningful implications? Are limitations acknowledged? Does the work advance knowledge?
D
Clarity & Presentation — 20 pts
Is the paper clearly written and well-structured? Are figures and tables informative and properly labeled?

Prizes & Awards

Prizes are awarded per research track. The overall Grand Prix winner receives the highest honor of the competition.

  • Grand Prix: $15,000 + Gold Medal + Publication in ISRC Annual Journal
  • Track Gold: $5,000 + Gold Certificate + Publication recommendation
  • Track Silver: $2,500 + Silver Certificate
  • Track Bronze: $1,000 + Bronze Certificate
  • Honorable Mention: Certificate of Recognition
  • All Participants: Certificate of Participation

Academic Ethics Policy

ISRC maintains strict academic integrity standards. Violations may result in immediate disqualification and a ban from future editions.

  • Plagiarism of any kind — including self-plagiarism — is strictly prohibited
  • AI-generated text exceeding 10% of the paper must be disclosed
  • Human subjects research must provide evidence of IRB/ethics board approval
  • Data fabrication or falsification results in permanent disqualification and institutional notification
  • All submissions are processed through iThenticate for plagiarism detection
